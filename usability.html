Usability Testing


Team SAKA Collaborators:
Amy Shropshire
Jerome Azbell
Burns Kennedy
Joanna Averch

For this group project, we used a number of participants to examine a website in terms of ease of use and manuverability, as well as design issues. The website we will examine is St. Francis Animal Hospital

First we discussed the nature of the client website, with input from the client about the basis of their business, their goals for the business, and in what ways a survey could help them. We also used client input to deconstruct the main target audience for the website, and for our study. View our brief Bio of St. Francis, as well as our Target Audience information.

After the initial planning stage, we created a three part survey, designed to elicit responses based on the client's goals and questions regarding the usability of their site. See our Entrance Questionnaire for questions we asked about basic demographics, as well as technological competencies, see our Main Survey for the task-based questionnaire we used to assess the usability of the site, and see our Exit Questionairre for follow-up questions about how the participants viewed the site after completing the survey.

The survey was administered primarily through social media. For details about the process of administering the survey, please see our Study Process Report as well as our Participant Waiver/Consent for details on how we protected privacy and confidentiality.

Finally, we analyzed the data into several charts and graphs, which we included in the Client Report along with our recommendations for site improvements. You can also view our spreadsheet with the full and complete de-identified Data. For our full breadth of our visual data analysis, see our Charts and Graphs.
